Session 3: Re-Designing Assessments of Learning for the Age of AI

Facilitator: Tari Tan
Location: H101 - 103 (Opened)

This interactive session focuses on redesigning assignments and assessments, so they remain meaningful, authentic, and effective in an era of generative AI. In the first part of the session, we will introduce frameworks to guide our thinking about assessments of learning and participants will reflect on assessments within their own teaching contexts to identify opportunities for re-design in an AI-native educational context. In the second part of the session, participants will gain hands-on practice using GenAI to begin to build out a re-designed assessment. Participants should bring their laptops or other devices and be prepared to access a GenAI tool of their choice, such as ChatGPT, Co-Pilot, or Gemini, to engage with the hands-on AI activities; however, pen-and-paper brainstorming will be available as an alternative.

Session 5: GenAI and Research Training: Integrating AI into Academic Workflows

Facilitators: Carlos Ponce and Tari Tan
Location: H101 - 103 (Opened)

This interactive session will engage participants in discussions that highlight opportunities and considerations for integrating GenAI tools and agents into graduate biomedical training. Participants will consider applications of GenAI within their own research and research training contexts and will have the opportunity to engage with GenAI tools to further explore the potential integration of GenAI into academic workflows. Participants should bring their laptops or other devices and be prepared to access a GenAI tool of their choice, such as ChatGPT, Co-Pilot, or Gemini, to engage with the hands-on AI activities; however, pen-and-paper brainstorming will be available as an alternative.
